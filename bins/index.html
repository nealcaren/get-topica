The idea, in one screen

A topic model sorts words into bins.

It reads a document, drops the filler, and drops every word that's left into one of a handful of topics. Watch it happen on three real survey answers — then read the bins.

idle press Sort the words to begin

Step 1 · prune

Throw away the filler

Words like the, and, about carry no topic. They're dropped before anything is counted — that's the greyed-out text falling away.

Step 2 · assign

Every word gets a bin

What's left is assigned to a topic. The model learns the bins from the data — nobody labels them by hand. Here they land as jobs · security, immigration · legal, and so on.

Step 3 · read

The bin is the topic

A topic is just its most-frequent words. Repeated words stack taller, so the leaders name the theme. That's what summary(model) prints — one line per bin.
